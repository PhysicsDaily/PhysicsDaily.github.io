Numerical Problems

Chapter 2: One-Dimensional Kinematics

Problem 1: Accelerating Car

A sports car starts from rest and accelerates uniformly at 4.0 m/s² for 10 seconds. Find (a) its final velocity and (b) the distance it travels in this time.

View Solution
Given: Initial velocity (v₀) = 0 m/s, acceleration (a) = 4.0 m/s², time (t) = 10 s.
(a) Final Velocity: We use the kinematic equation v = v₀ + at.

v = 0 m/s + (4.0 m/s²)(10 s) = 40 m/s.

(b) Distance Traveled: We use the kinematic equation x = v₀t + ½at².

x = (0 m/s)(10 s) + ½(4.0 m/s²)(10 s)²

x = 0 + (2.0 m/s²)(100 s²) = 200 m.

Problem 2: Ball Thrown Upward

A ball is thrown vertically upward from the ground with an initial speed of 25 m/s. (a) How long does it take to reach its highest point? (b) What is the maximum height it reaches? (c) What is its velocity just before it hits the ground? (Use g = 9.8 m/s²).

View Solution
Given: Initial velocity (v₀) = +25 m/s, acceleration (a) = -9.8 m/s². At the highest point, final velocity (v) = 0 m/s.
(a) Time to Reach Highest Point: Use v = v₀ + at.

0 = 25 m/s + (-9.8 m/s²)t

t = -25 m/s / -9.8 m/s² ≈ 2.55 s.

(b) Maximum Height: Use v² = v₀² + 2aΔy.

0² = (25 m/s)² + 2(-9.8 m/s²)Δy

0 = 625 - 19.6 Δy

Δy = -625 / -19.6 ≈ 31.9 m.

(c) Final Velocity: Due to symmetry in projectile motion (neglecting air resistance), the ball will hit the ground with the same magnitude of velocity as it was thrown, but in the opposite direction. Therefore, the final velocity is -25 m/s.
Problem 3: Braking Train

A train traveling at 30 m/s applies its brakes and comes to a stop in 15 seconds. Assuming the acceleration is constant, what is the train's acceleration and how far did it travel during braking?

View Solution
Given: Initial velocity (v₀) = 30 m/s, final velocity (v) = 0 m/s, time (t) = 15 s.
(a) Acceleration: Use v = v₀ + at.

0 m/s = 30 m/s + a(15 s)

a = -30 m/s / 15 s = -2.0 m/s². (The negative sign indicates deceleration).

(b) Distance Traveled: Use x = ½(v₀ + v)t.

x = ½(30 m/s + 0 m/s)(15 s)

x = ½(30 m/s)(15 s) = 225 m.

Problem 4: Catch-up Race

A police car at rest is passed by a speeding car traveling at a constant 40 m/s. At the instant the speeder passes, the police car starts from rest and accelerates at 5 m/s². How long does it take for the police car to catch the speeder?

View Solution
Strategy: The police car catches the speeder when they have both traveled the same distance from the starting point. We need to write position equations for both vehicles and set them equal.
Speeder's Position: The speeder moves at a constant velocity, so its position is given by x_s = v_s * t = 40t.
Police Car's Position: The police car accelerates from rest, so its position is given by x_p = v₀t + ½at² = 0 + ½(5)t² = 2.5t².
Set Positions Equal: We set x_p = x_s to find the time they meet.

2.5t² = 40t

Since t cannot be 0 (that's when they start), we can divide by t.

2.5t = 40

t = 40 / 2.5 = 16 s.

It takes the police car 16 seconds to catch the speeder.

Problem 5: Dropping a Stone in a Well

A stone is dropped into a deep well. The sound of the splash is heard 3.0 seconds after the stone is released. How deep is the well? (Assume the speed of sound in air is 343 m/s and g = 9.8 m/s²).

View Solution
Strategy: The total time of 3.0 s is the sum of the time it takes for the stone to fall (t₁) and the time it takes for the sound to travel back up (t₂). So, t₁ + t₂ = 3.0 s.
Time to Fall (t₁): Let the depth of the well be d. For the falling stone, d = ½gt₁². So, t₁ = √(2d/g).
Time for Sound (t₂): Sound travels at a constant speed, so d = v_sound * t₂. So, t₂ = d / v_sound.
Combine and Solve: Substitute the expressions for t₁ and t₂ into the total time equation:

√(2d/9.8) + d/343 = 3.0

This equation is complex to solve directly. A common approximation for such problems is to first calculate the depth ignoring the time for sound, and then refine. Let's assume t₁ ≈ 3.0 s.

Approximate depth: d ≈ ½(9.8)(3.0)² = 44.1 m.

Now, let's find a more precise answer. Let √d = x. The equation is √(2/9.8) * x + x²/343 = 3.0, which is a quadratic equation in x.

0.452x + 0.002915x² = 3.0

0.002915x² + 0.452x - 3.0 = 0

Using the quadratic formula, x ≈ 6.42. Therefore, d = x² ≈ 41.2 m.

Problem 6: Multi-Stage Motion

A subway train starts from rest at a station and accelerates at a rate of 1.60 m/s² for 14.0 s. It then runs at a constant speed for 70.0 s and finally decelerates at 3.50 m/s² until it stops at the next station. Find the total distance covered.

View Solution
Strategy: Break the motion into three stages and calculate the distance for each stage.
Stage 1 (Acceleration): v₀=0, a₁=1.60, t₁=14.0

Distance x₁ = ½a₁t₁² = ½(1.60)(14.0)² = 156.8 m.

Final velocity v₁ = a₁t₁ = (1.60)(14.0) = 22.4 m/s.

Stage 2 (Constant Velocity): v₂=22.4, t₂=70.0

Distance x₂ = v₂t₂ = (22.4)(70.0) = 1568 m.

Stage 3 (Deceleration): v₀=22.4, v_f=0, a₃=-3.50

Use v_f² = v₀² + 2a₃x₃.

0² = (22.4)² + 2(-3.50)x₃

x₃ = -(22.4)² / (2 * -3.50) = 501.76 / 7.0 ≈ 71.68 m.

Total Distance: X_total = x₁ + x₂ + x₃ = 156.8 + 1568 + 71.68 = 1796.48 m.
Problem 7: Free Fall from a Moving Balloon

A hot air balloon is ascending at a rate of 12 m/s and is 80 m above the ground when a package is dropped over the side. (a) How long does the package take to reach the ground? (b) With what speed does it hit the ground?

View Solution
Strategy: The package's initial velocity is the same as the balloon's velocity. We define "up" as positive.
Given: Initial velocity (v₀) = +12 m/s, initial height (y₀) = +80 m, final height (y) = 0 m, acceleration (a) = -9.8 m/s².
(a) Time to Reach Ground: Use y = y₀ + v₀t + ½at².

0 = 80 + 12t + ½(-9.8)t²

This gives the quadratic equation: 4.9t² - 12t - 80 = 0.

Using the quadratic formula, t = [12 ± √((-12)² - 4*4.9*(-80))] / (2*4.9).

t = [12 ± √(144 + 1568)] / 9.8 = [12 ± √1712] / 9.8 = [12 ± 41.38] / 9.8.

Since time must be positive, t = (12 + 41.38) / 9.8 ≈ 5.45 s.

(b) Final Speed: Use v = v₀ + at.

v = 12 m/s + (-9.8 m/s²)(5.45 s) = 12 - 53.41 = -41.41 m/s.

The speed is the magnitude, so it hits the ground at approximately 41.4 m/s.

Problem 8: Calculus-Based Motion

The position of a particle moving along the x-axis is given by x(t) = 2.0t³ - 9.0t² + 12.0t, where x is in meters and t is in seconds. (a) Find the times at which the particle is momentarily at rest. (b) What is the particle's acceleration at these times?

View Solution
Strategy: Find the velocity function by taking the first derivative of the position function. Find the acceleration function by taking the derivative of the velocity function.
(a) Find Velocity and Set to Zero:

Velocity v(t) = dx/dt = d/dt (2t³ - 9t² + 12t) = 6t² - 18t + 12.

Set v(t) = 0 to find when it is at rest: 6t² - 18t + 12 = 0.

Divide by 6: t² - 3t + 2 = 0.

Factor the quadratic: (t-1)(t-2) = 0.

The particle is at rest at t = 1.0 s and t = 2.0 s.

(b) Find Acceleration:

Acceleration a(t) = dv/dt = d/dt (6t² - 18t + 12) = 12t - 18.

At t = 1.0 s: a(1) = 12(1) - 18 = -6.0 m/s².

At t = 2.0 s: a(2) = 12(2) - 18 = 24 - 18 = +6.0 m/s².

Problem 9: Meeting Point in Free Fall

A ball is thrown upward from the ground with a speed of 30 m/s. At the same instant, another ball is dropped from rest from a building 45 m high. After how long and at what height will the balls collide?

View Solution
Strategy: Write position equations for both balls and set them equal to find the collision time and height. Let the ground be y=0.
Position of Ball 1 (Thrown Up): v₀₁ = +30 m/s, y₀₁ = 0.

y₁(t) = y₀₁ + v₀₁t + ½at² = 30t - 4.9t²

Position of Ball 2 (Dropped): v₀₂ = 0 m/s, y₀₂ = +45 m.

y₂(t) = y₀₂ + v₀₂t + ½at² = 45 - 4.9t²

Set Positions Equal: Collision occurs when y₁(t) = y₂(t).

30t - 4.9t² = 45 - 4.9t²

The -4.9t² terms cancel out.

30t = 45

t = 45 / 30 = 1.5 s. The balls collide after 1.5 seconds.

Find Collision Height: Substitute t = 1.5 s into either position equation.

y₁(1.5) = 30(1.5) - 4.9(1.5)² = 45 - 4.9(2.25) = 45 - 11.0 = 34.0 m.

Verification: y₂(1.5) = 45 - 4.9(1.5)² = 45 - 11.0 = 34.0 m ✓

Physical Check: The thrown ball reaches maximum height at t = 30/9.8 = 3.06 s with max height = 45.9 m, so collision at 34.0 m after 1.5 s is physically reasonable.

Problem 10: Distance in the nth Second

A car starting from rest accelerates at 2.0 m/s². Find the distance it travels during the 5th second of its motion.

View Solution
Strategy: The distance traveled in the 5th second is the difference between the distance traveled in 5 seconds and the distance traveled in 4 seconds.
Distance in 5 seconds (x₅):

x₅ = v₀t + ½at² = 0 + ½(2.0)(5.0)² = 25.0 m.

Distance in 4 seconds (x₄):

x₄ = v₀t + ½at² = 0 + ½(2.0)(4.0)² = 16.0 m.

Distance in the 5th second:

Δx = x₅ - x₄ = 25.0 m - 16.0 m = 9.0 m.

Alternative Formula: The distance in the nth second is given by S_n = u + a/2 (2n-1).

S₅ = 0 + (2.0/2)(2*5 - 1) = 1(9) = 9.0 m.

Problem 11: Reaction Time and Braking

A driver is traveling at 25 m/s when she sees an obstacle 80 m ahead. Her reaction time is 0.50 s, and her car's maximum deceleration is 6.0 m/s². Does she hit the obstacle?

View Solution
Strategy: Calculate the total stopping distance, which is the sum of the distance traveled during her reaction time and the distance traveled while braking.
Distance during reaction time (x_react): The car moves at a constant speed during this time.

x_react = v * t_react = (25 m/s)(0.50 s) = 12.5 m.

Distance while braking (x_brake): Use the kinematic equation v² = v₀² + 2ax.

Here, v₀ = 25 m/s, v = 0 m/s, and a = -6.0 m/s².

0² = (25)² + 2(-6.0)x_brake

0 = 625 - 12x_brake

x_brake = 625 / 12 ≈ 52.1 m.

Total Stopping Distance:

x_total = x_react + x_brake = 12.5 m + 52.1 m = 64.6 m.

Conclusion: Since the total stopping distance (64.6 m) is less than the distance to the obstacle (80 m), she does not hit the obstacle.
Problem 12: Average Speed vs. Average Velocity

A person walks 70 m east in 40 s, then turns around and walks 30 m west in 20 s. For the entire trip, find the person's (a) average speed and (b) average velocity.

View Solution
Strategy: Average speed depends on total distance, while average velocity depends on total displacement.
Calculate Totals:

Total distance = 70 m + 30 m = 100 m.

Total time = 40 s + 20 s = 60 s.

Total displacement = (+70 m) + (-30 m) = +40 m (or 40 m East).

(a) Average Speed:

Average Speed = Total Distance / Total Time

= 100 m / 60 s ≈ 1.67 m/s.

(b) Average Velocity:

Average Velocity = Total Displacement / Total Time

= +40 m / 60 s ≈ +0.67 m/s (or 0.67 m/s East).

Problem 13: Elevator Ride

An elevator starts from rest and accelerates upward at 1.5 m/s² for 4 seconds. It then continues to move upward at a constant velocity for 8 seconds. Finally, it decelerates at 2.0 m/s² until it comes to rest. Find the total height the elevator ascended.

View Solution
Strategy: This is a three-part motion problem. We calculate the distance for each part and add them together.
Part 1: Acceleration

Distance y₁ = v₀t + ½at² = 0 + ½(1.5)(4)² = 12 m.

Velocity at end of part 1: v₁ = v₀ + at = 0 + 1.5(4) = 6.0 m/s.

Part 2: Constant Velocity

Distance y₂ = vt = (6.0 m/s)(8 s) = 48 m.

Part 3: Deceleration

Use v² = v₀² + 2ay₃. Here v₀ = 6.0 m/s, v = 0, a = -2.0 m/s².

0² = (6.0)² + 2(-2.0)y₃

y₃ = -36 / -4 = 9 m.

Total Height:

Y_total = y₁ + y₂ + y₃ = 12 m + 48 m + 9 m = 69 m.

Problem 14: Dropped and Thrown Stones

From the edge of a 50 m high cliff, stone A is dropped from rest. At the same time, stone B is thrown vertically downward with a speed of 10 m/s. How much earlier does stone B hit the ground than stone A?

View Solution
Strategy: Calculate the time of flight for each stone using the kinematic equation y = y₀ + v₀t + ½at². Let the ground be y=0 and the clifftop be y=50.
Time for Stone A (dropped): v₀ = 0, y₀ = 50, y = 0, a = -9.8.

0 = 50 + 0*t_A - 4.9t_A²

t_A² = 50 / 4.9 ≈ 10.2

t_A ≈ 3.19 s.

Time for Stone B (thrown): v₀ = -10, y₀ = 50, y = 0, a = -9.8.

0 = 50 - 10*t_B - 4.9t_B²

4.9t_B² + 10t_B - 50 = 0

Using the quadratic formula, t_B = [-10 ± √(10² - 4*4.9*(-50))] / (2*4.9).

t_B = [-10 ± √(100 + 980)] / 9.8 = [-10 ± √1080] / 9.8 = [-10 ± 32.86] / 9.8.

Taking the positive root, t_B = 22.86 / 9.8 ≈ 2.33 s.

Time Difference:

Δt = t_A - t_B = 3.19 s - 2.33 s = 0.86 s.

Stone B hits the ground 0.86 seconds earlier than stone A.

Problem 15: Position-Dependent Acceleration

A particle starts from rest at x = 1 m and moves along the x-axis with an acceleration given by a(x) = 4x m/s². What is its speed when it reaches x = 3 m?

View Solution
Strategy: Since acceleration is a function of position, not time, we must use the integral form relating acceleration, velocity, and position, which comes from the chain rule: a = dv/dt = (dv/dx)(dx/dt) = v(dv/dx).
Set up the Integral:

We rearrange to a dx = v dv and integrate both sides from the initial to the final state.

∫a(x) dx from x₀ to x_f = ∫v dv from v₀ to v_f.

Perform the Integration:

Initial conditions: x₀ = 1, v₀ = 0. Final conditions: x_f = 3, v_f = ?

∫(4x) dx from 1 to 3 = ∫v dv from 0 to v_f.

[2x²] from 1 to 3 = [½v²] from 0 to v_f.

2(3)² - 2(1)² = ½v_f² - 0

18 - 2 = ½v_f²

16 = ½v_f²

v_f² = 32

v_f = √32 ≈ 5.66 m/s.
