Significant Figures

Rules for determining and using significant figures in measurements and calculations

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Identify significant figures in measurements and calculated values
  • Apply the rules for determining significant figures
  • Perform arithmetic operations with proper significant figure handling
  • Understand the relationship between precision and significant figures
  • Round calculated results to the appropriate number of significant figures

🔢 Rules for Significant Figures

Identifying Significant Figures

✓ Always Significant

  • All non-zero digits: 123 → 3 sig figs
  • Zeros between non-zero digits: 105 → 3 sig figs
  • Trailing zeros with decimal point: 2.50 → 3 sig figs

⚠️ Sometimes Significant

  • Leading zeros: 0.025 → 2 sig figs (not significant)
  • Trailing zeros without decimal: 1200 → ambiguous (2 or 4?)

Arithmetic Operations

Addition & Subtraction

Round to the least precise decimal place

12.1 + 0.35 = 12.45
→ Round to 12.5

Multiplication & Division

Round to the least number of sig figs

2.4 × 1.25 = 3.0
→ 2 sig figs (from 2.4)

💡 Example: Mixed Operations

Calculate: (12.1 + 0.35) × 2.4

Step 1: Addition first
12.1 + 0.35 = 12.45 → 12.5 (1 decimal place)

Step 2: Multiplication
12.5 × 2.4 = 30.0 → 30 (2 sig figs)
