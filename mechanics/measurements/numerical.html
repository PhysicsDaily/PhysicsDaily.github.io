Chapter 1: Measurement

Apply your knowledge to solve quantitative problems and sharpen your calculation skills.

🧮 Numerical Problems

1. The speed of light is 299,792,458 m/s. Convert this to:
  • a) kilometers per hour (km/h)
  • b) miles per hour (mph) [1 mile = 1.609 km]
Answer

a) 299,792,458 m/s × (3600 s/h) × (1 km/1000 m) = 1.079 × 10⁹ km/h

b) 1.079 × 10⁹ km/h × (1 mi/1.609 km) = 6.71 × 10⁸ mph

2. The period of a simple pendulum is given by T = 2π√(L/g). Use dimensional analysis to verify this relationship.
Answer

Assume T ∝ Lᵃ gᵇ. Dimensionally: [T] = [L]ᵃ [LT⁻²]ᵇ = [L^(a+b) T^(-2b)].

Comparing exponents: For T: 1 = -2b ⇒ b = -1/2. For L: 0 = a+b ⇒ a = -b = 1/2.

So, T ∝ L^(1/2) g^(-1/2) ⇒ T ∝ √(L/g). The equation is dimensionally consistent. The constant 2π is dimensionless and cannot be found this way.

3. Verify dimensional consistency for Einstein's mass-energy equation E = mc².
Answer

LHS: Dimension of Energy [E] = [ML²T⁻²].

RHS: Dimension of [mc²] = [M][LT⁻¹]² = [M][L²T⁻²] = [ML²T⁻²].

✅ Both sides have the same dimension, so the equation is consistent.

4. A rectangular swimming pool measures 25.0 m × 12.5 m × 2.0 m. Calculate its volume and the mass of the water it holds (density of water ≈ 1000 kg/m³), respecting significant figures.
Answer

Volume: V = 25.0 × 12.5 × 2.0 = 625 m³. Since 2.0 has two sig figs, we should report the volume as 6.2 × 10² m³.

Mass: m = ρV = (1000 kg/m³)(625 m³) = 625,000 kg = 6.2 × 10⁵ kg.

5. The period of a mass-spring system is T = 2π√(m/k). What are the dimensions of the spring constant k?
Answer

From the formula, T² ∝ m/k, so k ∝ m/T².

Dimensions of k: [k] = [M]/[T²] = [MT⁻²]. This is consistent with the unit of force per unit length (N/m).

6. Perform the following calculations with proper significant figures:
  • a) 23.45 + 1.2 + 0.678
  • b) 4.56 × 2.1
  • c) (2.34 × 10³) ÷ (1.2 × 10⁻⁴)
Answer

a) 23.45 + 1.2 + 0.678 = 25.328 → 25.3 (limited by 1.2, which has one decimal place).

b) 4.56 × 2.1 = 9.576 → 9.6 (limited by 2.1, which has two significant figures).

c) (2.34 × 10³) ÷ (1.2 × 10⁻⁴) = 1.95 × 10⁷ → 1.9 × 10⁷ (limited by 1.2, which has two significant figures).
