🧮 Numerical Problems

Problem 1 Easy

A student measures the sides of a rectangular block as l = 15.12 cm, w = 3.45 cm, and h = 1.78 cm. Calculate the volume of the block, expressing the answer with the correct number of significant figures.

Solution

Step 1: Identify the operation and rule

The volume V is calculated by multiplication: V = l × w × h. The rule for multiplication is that the result must have the same number of significant figures as the measurement with the fewest significant figures.

Step 2: Count significant figures

  • l = 15.12 cm has 4 significant figures.
  • w = 3.45 cm has 3 significant figures.
  • h = 1.78 cm has 3 significant figures.

The result must be rounded to 3 significant figures.

Step 3: Calculate the volume

V = 15.12 cm × 3.45 cm × 1.78 cm = 92.78592 cm³

Step 4: Round to the correct number of significant figures

Rounding 92.78592 to 3 significant figures gives 92.8.

Final Answer: The volume is 92.8 cm³.

Problem 2 Easy

Convert the following measurements to the specified units:

  • 2.34 km to meters
  • 0.00789 g to milligrams
  • 4.56 × 10⁻⁴ m to micrometers
Solution

Part A: Convert 2.34 km to meters

Since 1 km = 1000 m:

2.34 km × (1000 m/1 km) = 2340 m

Part B: Convert 0.00789 g to milligrams

Since 1 g = 1000 mg:

0.00789 g × (1000 mg/1 g) = 7.89 mg

Part C: Convert 4.56 × 10⁻⁴ m to micrometers

Since 1 m = 10⁶ μm:

4.56 × 10⁻⁴ m × (10⁶ μm/1 m) = 456 μm

Final Answers:

  • 2.34 km = 2340 m
  • 0.00789 g = 7.89 mg
  • 4.56 × 10⁻⁴ m = 456 μm
Problem 3 Medium

A cylindrical container has a diameter of 12.5 ± 0.1 cm and a height of 8.7 ± 0.1 cm. Calculate the volume of the container with its uncertainty.

Solution

Step 1: Calculate the radius

The radius is half the diameter: r = (12.5 ± 0.1)/2 = 6.25 ± 0.05 cm

Step 2: Apply the volume formula

For a cylinder: V = πr²h

V = π × (6.25)² × 8.7 = π × 39.0625 × 8.7 = 1069.0 cm³

Step 3: Calculate the uncertainty

For multiplication/division with uncertainties:

δV/V = √[(2δr/r)² + (δh/h)²]

Where:

  • δr/r = 0.05/6.25 = 0.008
  • δh/h = 0.1/8.7 = 0.0115

δV/V = √[(2×0.008)² + (0.0115)²] = √[0.000256 + 0.000132] = 0.0197

δV = 1069.0 × 0.0197 = 21.1 cm³

Final Answer: The volume is (1069 ± 21) cm³

Problem 4 Medium

Using dimensional analysis, derive the units for gravitational potential energy if the formula is U = mgh, where m is mass, g is gravitational acceleration, and h is height.

Solution

Step 1: Identify the dimensions

  • Mass m: [M]
  • Gravitational acceleration g: [LT⁻²]
  • Height h: [L]

Step 2: Apply dimensional analysis

[U] = [m][g][h] = [M][LT⁻²][L] = [ML²T⁻²]

Step 3: Convert to SI units

The dimensions [ML²T⁻²] correspond to:

  • M → kg (kilograms)
  • L² → m² (square meters)
  • T⁻² → s⁻² (per second squared)

Final Answer: The units of gravitational potential energy are kg⋅m²⋅s⁻², which equals Joules (J)

Problem 5 Hard

A rectangular prism has measurements l = 8.4 ± 0.2 cm, w = 5.7 ± 0.2 cm, and h = 3.1 ± 0.1 cm. Calculate both the surface area and volume with their uncertainties.

Solution

Part A: Calculate Volume

V = l × w × h = 8.4 × 5.7 × 3.1 = 148.428 cm³ ≈ 148 cm³

For volume uncertainty (multiplication):

δV/V = √[(δl/l)² + (δw/w)² + (δh/h)²]

  • δl/l = 0.2/8.4 = 0.0238
  • δw/w = 0.2/5.7 = 0.0351
  • δh/h = 0.1/3.1 = 0.0323

δV/V = √[0.0238² + 0.0351² + 0.0323²] = 0.0562

δV = 148 × 0.0562 = 8.3 cm³ ≈ 8 cm³

Part B: Calculate Surface Area

SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)

SA = 2(8.4×5.7 + 8.4×3.1 + 5.7×3.1)

SA = 2(47.88 + 26.04 + 17.67) = 2(91.59) = 183.18 cm² ≈ 183 cm²

For surface area uncertainty, we use the general propagation formula for each term:

δ(SA) = 2√[(δ(lw))² + (δ(lh))² + (δ(wh))²]

Where each term follows: δ(xy) = xy√[(δx/x)² + (δy/y)²]

  • δ(lw) = 47.88 × √[0.0238² + 0.0351²] = 2.05 cm²
  • δ(lh) = 26.04 × √[0.0238² + 0.0323²] = 1.04 cm²
  • δ(wh) = 17.67 × √[0.0351² + 0.0323²] = 0.84 cm²

δ(SA) = 2√[2.05² + 1.04² + 0.84²] = 2 × 2.46 = 4.92 cm² ≈ 5 cm²

Surface Area: (183 ± 5) cm²

Volume: (148 ± 8) cm³
