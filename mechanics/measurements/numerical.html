🧮 Numerical Problems

Problem 1 Easy

Convert the following measurements to the specified units:

a) 2.5 km to meters

b) 750 mL to liters

c) 3.6 × 10⁴ s to hours

d) 55 mph to m/s

Solution

Step-by-step Solutions:

a) 2.5 km = 2.5 × 1000 m = 2500 m = 2.5 × 10³ m

b) 750 mL = 750 × 10⁻³ L = 0.750 L

c) 3.6 × 10⁴ s = 3.6 × 10⁴ s × (1 h / 3600 s) = 10 h

d) 55 mph = 55 × (1609.34 m / 1 mile) × (1 h / 3600 s) = 24.6 m/s

Problem 2 Easy

Express the following numbers in proper scientific notation with the correct number of significant figures:

a) 0.00456

b) 12,340,000

c) 0.0809

d) Calculate: (3.14159) × (2.0)

Solution

Solutions:

a) 4.56 × 10⁻³ (3 significant figures)

b) 1.234 × 10⁷ (4 significant figures, assuming trailing zeros are not significant)

c) 8.09 × 10⁻² (3 significant figures)

d) 6.3 (limited by 2.0 which has 2 significant figures)

Problem 3 Medium

Using dimensional analysis, derive the units for the following physical quantities:

a) Gravitational constant G in Newton's law: F = Gm₁m₂/r²

b) Spring constant k in Hooke's law: F = kx

c) Surface tension γ where force per unit length: F = γL

Solution

Dimensional Analysis:

a) From F = Gm₁m₂/r²:

[G] = [F][r²]/[m₁][m₂] = [MLT⁻²][L²]/[M²] = [M⁻¹L³T⁻²]

Units: m³ kg⁻¹ s⁻²

b) From F = kx:

[k] = [F]/[x] = [MLT⁻²]/[L] = [MT⁻²]

Units: kg s⁻² or N/m

c) From F = γL:

[γ] = [F]/[L] = [MLT⁻²]/[L] = [MT⁻²]

Units: kg s⁻² or N/m

Problem 4 Medium

The period of a simple pendulum depends on its length l, the acceleration due to gravity g, and possibly its mass m. Use dimensional analysis to find the relationship.

Given:

  • [T] = [T] (time dimension)
  • [l] = [L] (length dimension)
  • [g] = [LT⁻²] (acceleration dimension)
  • [m] = [M] (mass dimension)
Solution

Step 1: Assume the relationship

Let T = k × l^a × g^b × m^c where k is dimensionless

Step 2: Write dimensional equation

[T] = [L^a] × [LT⁻²]^b × [M^c]

[T] = [L^a] × [L^b T^(-2b)] × [M^c]

[T¹] = [L^(a+b) T^(-2b) M^c]

Step 3: Equate powers

For M: 0 = c → c = 0

For T: 1 = -2b → b = -1/2

For L: 0 = a + b → a = -b = 1/2

Result: T = k√(l/g)

The period is independent of mass and proportional to √(l/g)

Problem 5 Medium

A rectangular metal sheet has dimensions 2.50 m × 1.20 m and thickness 3.00 mm. Calculate:

a) The area of the sheet

b) The volume of the sheet

c) If the density is 7.85 × 10³ kg/m³, find the mass

Express all answers with proper significant figures.

Solution

Given data:

  • Length: l = 2.50 m (3 sig figs)
  • Width: w = 1.20 m (3 sig figs)
  • Thickness: t = 3.00 mm = 3.00 × 10⁻³ m (3 sig figs)
  • Density: ρ = 7.85 × 10³ kg/m³ (3 sig figs)

Solution:

a) Area:

A = l × w = 2.50 m × 1.20 m = 3.00 m²

b) Volume:

V = l × w × t = 2.50 m × 1.20 m × 3.00 × 10⁻³ m

V = 9.00 × 10⁻³ m³

c) Mass:

m = ρV = 7.85 × 10³ kg/m³ × 9.00 × 10⁻³ m³

m = 70.7 kg

Final Answers:

a) Area = 3.00 m²

b) Volume = 9.00 × 10⁻³ m³

c) Mass = 70.7 kg

Problem 6 Hard

The terminal velocity v of a sphere falling through a fluid depends on:

  • Sphere radius: r
  • Sphere density: ρₛ
  • Fluid density: ρf
  • Fluid viscosity: η (dimensions: [ML⁻¹T⁻¹])
  • Gravitational acceleration: g

Use dimensional analysis to derive the functional form of terminal velocity.

Solution

Step 1: Set up the relationship

Assume: v = k × r^a × ρₛ^b × ρf^c × η^d × g^e

Step 2: Write dimensions

[LT⁻¹] = [L^a] × [ML⁻³]^b × [ML⁻³]^c × [ML⁻¹T⁻¹]^d × [LT⁻²]^e

[LT⁻¹] = [L^a M^b L^(-3b) M^c L^(-3c) M^d L^(-d) T^(-d) L^e T^(-2e)]

[L¹T⁻¹] = [L^(a-3b-3c-d+e) M^(b+c+d) T^(-d-2e)]

Step 3: Equate powers

For M: 0 = b + c + d ... (1)

For T: -1 = -d - 2e ... (2)

For L: 1 = a - 3b - 3c - d + e ... (3)

Step 4: Solve the system

From (2): d + 2e = 1

From (1): c = -b - d

Substituting into (3): 1 = a - 3b - 3(-b - d) - d + e

1 = a - 3b + 3b + 3d - d + e = a + 2d + e

We have more unknowns than equations, so we assume the simplest case where the density difference matters:

Let b = 1, c = -1 (density difference effect)

From (1): d = -1 - (-1) = 0

From (2): e = 1/2

From (3): a = 1 - 2(0) - 1/2 = 1/2

But this gives d = 0, which ignores viscosity. Let's try d = 1:

From (2): e = 0

From (1): b + c = -1

From (3): a = 1 + 3b + 3c + 1 = 2 + 3(b + c) = 2 - 3 = -1

This gives negative power for radius, which is unphysical. The correct approach requires considering that (ρₛ - ρf) acts as a single quantity.

Result: v ∝ r²g(ρₛ - ρf)/η

This is consistent with Stokes' law: v = (2/9) × r²g(ρₛ - ρf)/η

Problem 7 Hard

Error Analysis Problem: A student measures the period of a pendulum multiple times and gets the following results (in seconds):

2.41, 2.38, 2.42, 2.39, 2.40, 2.43, 2.37, 2.41

Calculate:

a) The mean period

b) The standard deviation

c) The uncertainty in the mean

d) Express the result with proper uncertainty notation

Solution

Given measurements (in seconds):

2.41, 2.38, 2.42, 2.39, 2.40, 2.43, 2.37, 2.41

Number of measurements: n = 8

a) Mean period:

T̄ = (2.41 + 2.38 + 2.42 + 2.39 + 2.40 + 2.43 + 2.37 + 2.41)/8

T̄ = 19.21/8 = 2.401 s

b) Standard deviation:

First, calculate deviations from mean:

(Tᵢ - T̄)² values: 0.000081, 0.000441, 0.000361, 0.000121, 0.000001, 0.000841, 0.000961, 0.000081

σ = √[Σ(Tᵢ - T̄)²/(n-1)] = √(0.002888/7) = √0.000413 = 0.0203 s

c) Uncertainty in the mean:

σₘ = σ/√n = 0.0203/√8 = 0.0203/2.828 = 0.0072 s

d) Result with uncertainty:

Final Result: T = (2.401 ± 0.007) s

or T = 2.40 ± 0.01 s (rounded to appropriate precision)

Problem 8 Hard

Order of Magnitude Estimation: Estimate the number of atoms in a human body.

Useful Information:

  • Average human mass ≈ 70 kg
  • Human body is mostly water (H₂O)
  • Avogadro's number: Nₐ = 6.02 × 10²³ mol⁻¹
  • Atomic masses: H ≈ 1 u, O ≈ 16 u
  • Atomic mass unit: 1 u = 1.66 × 10⁻²⁷ kg
Solution

Approach: Use water as approximation

Step 1: Mass of water molecule

H₂O molecular mass = 2(1) + 16 = 18 u

Mass of one molecule = 18 × 1.66 × 10⁻²⁷ kg = 2.99 × 10⁻²⁶ kg

Step 2: Number of molecules in human body

N_molecules = 70 kg / (2.99 × 10⁻²⁶ kg) = 2.34 × 10²⁷ molecules

Step 3: Number of atoms

Each H₂O molecule has 3 atoms (2 H + 1 O)

N_atoms = 3 × 2.34 × 10²⁷ = 7.02 × 10²⁷ atoms

Alternative approach using moles:

Moles of H₂O = 70 kg / (0.018 kg/mol) = 3.89 × 10³ mol

Molecules = 3.89 × 10³ × 6.02 × 10²³ = 2.34 × 10²⁷

Atoms = 3 × 2.34 × 10²⁷ = 7.02 × 10²⁷

Order of Magnitude: ~10²⁸ atoms

More precise estimate: 7 × 10²⁷ atoms
