1. The speed of light is 299,792,458 m/s. Convert this to:
  • a) kilometers per hour (km/h)
  • b) miles per hour (mph) [1 mile = 1.609 km]
Answer

a) $299,792,458 \, \text{m/s} \times (3600 \, \text{s/h}) \times (1 \, \text{km}/1000 \, \text{m}) = \mathbf{1.079 \times 10^9 \, \text{km/h}}$

b) $1.079 \times 10^9 \, \text{km/h} \times (1 \, \text{mi}/1.609 \, \text{km}) = \mathbf{6.71 \times 10^8 \, \text{mph}}$

2. The period of a simple pendulum is given by $T = 2\pi\sqrt{L/g}$. Use dimensional analysis to verify this relationship.
Answer

Assume $T \propto L^a g^b$. Dimensionally: $[T] = [L]^a [LT^{-2}]^b = [L^{a+b} T^{-2b}]$.

Comparing exponents: For T: $1 = -2b \implies b = -1/2$. For L: $0 = a+b \implies a = -b = 1/2$.

So, $T \propto L^{1/2} g^{-1/2} \implies T \propto \sqrt{L/g}$. The equation is dimensionally consistent. The constant $2\pi$ is dimensionless and cannot be found this way.

3. Verify dimensional consistency for Einstein's mass-energy equation $E = mc^2$.
Answer

LHS: Dimension of Energy $[E] = [ML^2T^{-2}]$.

RHS: Dimension of $[mc^2] = [M][LT^{-1}]^2 = [M][L^2T^{-2}] = [ML^2T^{-2}]$.

‚úÖ Both sides have the same dimension, so the equation is consistent.

4. A rectangular swimming pool measures 25.0 m √ó 12.5 m √ó 2.0 m. Calculate its volume and the mass of the water it holds (density of water ‚âà 1000 kg/m¬≥), respecting significant figures.
Answer

Volume: $V = 25.0 \times 12.5 \times 2.0 = 625 \, m^3$. Since 2.0 has two sig figs, we should report the volume as $\mathbf{6.2 \times 10^2 \, m^3}$.

Mass: $m = \rho V = (1000 \, \text{kg/m}^3)(625 \, \text{m}^3) = 625,000 \, \text{kg} = \mathbf{6.2 \times 10^5 \, \text{kg}}$.

5. The period of a mass-spring system is $T = 2\pi\sqrt{m/k}$. What are the dimensions of the spring constant $k$?
Answer

From the formula, $T^2 \propto m/k$, so $k \propto m/T^2$.

Dimensions of $k$: $[k] = [M]/[T^2] = \mathbf{[MT^{-2}]}$. This is consistent with the unit of force per unit length (N/m).

6. Perform the following calculations with proper significant figures:
  • a) $23.45 + 1.2 + 0.678$
  • b) $4.56 \times 2.1$
  • c) $(2.34 \times 10^3) \div (1.2 \times 10^{-4})$
Answer

a) $23.45 + 1.2 + 0.678 = 25.328 \rightarrow \mathbf{25.3}$ (limited by 1.2, which has one decimal place).

b) $4.56 \times 2.1 = 9.576 \rightarrow \mathbf{9.6}$ (limited by 2.1, which has two significant figures).

c) $(2.34 \times 10^3) \div (1.2 \times 10^{-4}) = 1.95 \times 10^7 \rightarrow \mathbf{1.9 \times 10^7}$ (limited by 1.2, which has two significant figures).
